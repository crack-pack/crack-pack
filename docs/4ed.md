# Fourth Edition (`4ed`) — known gaps & assumptions

Part of [crack-pack](../README.md) · see the [status matrix](../ASSUMPTIONS.md#status-matrix).

**Status:** common/uncommon ✅ validated · rare sheet 🟡 reconstructed from Revised · collation 🟡 assumed.

## What's solid
- **Common and uncommon sheets are real** and validated (121 distinct each; the
  source shows two common-sheet versions — we use "version 1"). Basic lands
  (three A/B/C variants) are on a separate land sheet and don't appear in
  boosters, so these are pure card grids. Pack = 11 commons + 3 uncommons + 1 rare.

## The gap
- **No transcribed rare sheet.** The Collation Project page has no rare-sheet
  gallery — only prose noting that *French* and *Portuguese* sheet images exist,
  and that the author's data is "mostly consistent with the French sheet" but has
  "some irregularities."

## What we assumed — reconstructed from Revised
- 4th Edition is a direct evolution of **Revised**, whose rare sheet we *do* have.
  So the `4ed` rare grid is the **real Revised rare sheet** with substitutions:
  - **90 of the 121 rares carried over** from Revised and keep their Revised grid positions.
  - The **31 rares new to 4th Edition** are placed into the slots vacated by the
    31 removed Revised rares (these 31 positions are assumed).
- This is plausible but **unverified** — the real 4th Edition sheet may differ,
  and even carried-over cards may have moved.
