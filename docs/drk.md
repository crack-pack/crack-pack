# The Dark (`drk`) — known gaps & assumptions

Part of [crack-pack](../README.md) · see the [status matrix](../ASSUMPTIONS.md#status-matrix).

**Status:** sheet data ✅ validated · collation 🔴 simplified.

## What's solid
- Two 11×11 sheets (common + uncommon; no rare sheet), grids checksum-validated:
  common 40×C3 + Maze of Ith ×1; uncommon 43×U2 + 35×U1 (the 35 U1s are the
  de-facto rares).

## The gap
- The Dark's **real collation is variable per box** — there is no single
  deterministic walk to reproduce. Per the source: sheets may or may not split
  (common as top-5 / bottom-6), a box carries *"2 or 3 independent sequences of
  commons,"* and non-split boxes show width-7 stripes.

## What we assumed
- A plain two-sheet striped model with stripe cycle **`[2,3,4,5]`**. This is a
  deliberate **simplification** of an inherently non-deterministic process — the
  grids are real, the pack grouping is a stand-in, not one "correct" answer.

## How you can help
- Box openings that characterise the split / multi-sequence behaviour — enough to
  decide whether a better (even if still approximate) model is worthwhile.
- [Open an issue or PR](https://github.com/crack-pack/crack-pack/issues).
